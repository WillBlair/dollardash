Solo Story Mode — Chapter Flow

A young graduate's journey into the world of finance. 3 demo chapters, extensible architecture.

The Character

You just graduated college. You've got your first real job, your first paycheck... and zero idea what to do with your money. Your mentor (the narrator/AI voice) guides you through each chapter.

Chapter Structure (each chapter follows this pattern)

📖
Story Beat

Narrative setup — what's happening in your life

🎓
Learn

Quick concept lesson with visuals

🎮
Play

Guided mini-round applying the concept

⭐
Reflect

Results + "what you learned" summary

The 3 Demo Chapters

1

Chapter 1 — "First Paycheck"

Story: It's your first month at your new job. You open your bank app and see real money for the first time. Your mentor says: "Let's make that money work for you."

Concept: What is a stock? Buying a share = owning a tiny piece of a company. Price goes up, you profit. Price goes down, you lose.

Gameplay: Simplified round — only 1 stock available. Buy, watch it move, sell. Experience the basic loop with training wheels on.

Takeaway: "You just made your first trade. Stocks are ownership — when the company does well, so do you."

2

Chapter 2 — "Reading the Room"

Story: A few weeks in, your coworker mentions they made money because they read the news before everyone else. Your mentor explains: "Markets react to information."

Concept: How news affects stock prices. Bullish = good news = price goes up. Bearish = bad news = price goes down. Smart traders read the signals.

Gameplay: 2 stocks unlocked, news ticker is active. Headlines flash — decide whether to buy or sell based on what you read. The game highlights which news matters for which stock.

Takeaway: "Information is power. The news feed isn't decoration — it's your edge."

3

Chapter 3 — "Don't Bet It All on One Horse"

Story: You went all-in on one stock and it crashed. Your mentor shakes their head: "Let me teach you about diversification." You start over with a lesson learned.

Concept: Diversification — spreading risk across multiple stocks with different risk levels. Volatile vs. stable. Portfolio balance.

Gameplay: All 4 stocks unlocked. Full trading sim. A crash event is scripted mid-round to teach the lesson viscerally. Players who diversified survive; those who didn't feel the pain.

Takeaway: "Diversification isn't exciting — it's survival. You're ready for the real thing." → Unlocks multiplayer.

🏁 After Chapter 3 → Multiplayer Unlocked

"You've graduated from training. Time to compete against real traders." → Routes to the existing Host/Join multiplayer flow, where all concepts come together in a live competitive round.
