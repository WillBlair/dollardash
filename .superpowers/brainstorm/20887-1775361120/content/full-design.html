Dollar Dash — Story Mode Design

Full design proposal. Review each section — click any that needs changes.

Overview

Two-Mode Architecture

🎓 Solo: Story Mode

A narrative-driven tutorial where Dollar Guy guides a young person through their first financial experiences. 3 chapters, each teaching a core concept through story → lesson → guided play → reflection.

Replaces the current solo free-play. Current solo sim mechanics are reused inside chapter gameplay rounds.

⚔️ Multiplayer: Competitive

The existing Host/Join multiplayer — unchanged. Players who completed Story Mode understand the concepts; those who didn't can still jump in but won't know the strategies.

Story Mode "unlocks" multiplayer narratively, but no hard gate — anyone can still join a room code.

The Story

Premise

You're a young person who just got your first real job and your first paycheck. You know nothing about money, investing, or markets. Dollar Guy — a pixel-art Wall Street character — appears and becomes your guide. Over 3 chapters he takes you from "what even is a stock" to "I can compete with real traders."

Chapter Pattern

Every chapter follows this 4-phase structure:

Phase 1
Story Beat

Dollar Guy narrates via speech bubbles. Sets up the real-life situation. Player clicks through dialog.

Phase 2
Learn

Dollar Guy teaches the concept. Mix of speech bubbles + 1-2 interactive moments (tap to buy, drag to allocate, quick quiz).

Phase 3
Play

Guided trading round with chapter-specific objectives. Dollar Guy gives hints (fading over chapters). Uses existing sim engine.

Phase 4
Reflect

Results screen: did you meet the objective? Dollar Guy summarizes what you learned. Badge earned. Next chapter unlocks.

The 3 Chapters

Chapter 1 — "First Paycheck"

StoryYou got your first paycheck from your new job. You're staring at your bank account thinking "now what?" Dollar Guy pops up: "Hey! Congrats on the first check. Wanna make that money actually do something? Let me show you."
ConceptWhat is a stock? Buying a share = owning a piece of a company. Price goes up → profit. Price goes down → loss. That's it.
InteractiveTap to "buy your first share" of a single stock. Watch the price move in real time. Tap to sell when you're ready.
Gameplay1 stock unlocked, 60 seconds. Objective: "Buy 1 share and sell it for a profit." Dollar Guy gives heavy hints: "Price is going up — nice time to sell!"
Takeaway"You just made your first trade! A stock is just a piece of a company. When it does well, so do you."

Chapter 2 — "Reading the Room"

StoryA coworker brags about making money because they saw a news headline before everyone else. Dollar Guy: "Markets react to information. The trick is knowing what to look for."
ConceptNews moves markets. Bullish news → price up. Bearish news → price down. Reading headlines = your edge.
InteractiveShown 3 headlines — tap whether each is bullish 📈 or bearish 📉. Dollar Guy confirms/corrects. Quick and punchy.
Gameplay2 stocks unlocked, 90 seconds, news ticker active. Objective: "Make at least one trade based on a news headline." Dollar Guy highlights relevant headlines with moderate hints.
Takeaway"Information is power. The news isn't just noise — it's your cheat code."

Chapter 3 — "Don't Bet It All"

StoryDollar Guy tells a cautionary tale: "I once knew a guy who put everything into one stock. It crashed. He lost it all. Don't be that guy."
ConceptDiversification. Don't put all eggs in one basket. Spread across stocks with different risk levels. Volatile vs. stable.
InteractiveDrag-to-allocate: given $100, split it across 4 stocks. See a simulated outcome showing how diversified vs. concentrated portfolios perform in a crash.
GameplayAll 4 stocks, 2 minutes, scripted crash event mid-round. Objective: "End the round with shares in at least 3 different stocks." Minimal hints — Dollar Guy trusts you now.
Takeaway"Diversification isn't exciting, but it keeps you alive. You're ready for the real thing."
After Story Mode

🏁 Story Complete → Multiplayer Unlocked

Dollar Guy: "You've graduated from training. Think you can beat real people? Let's find out."

Player is routed to the Home Screen with multiplayer now emphasized. The HOST/JOIN buttons are the clear next step. All 4 stocks, news, no hints — everything they learned is now their toolkit.

No hard gate — multiplayer is always accessible via room code. But the story creates a natural "earn your way there" progression.

Technical Approach

How it maps to the existing codebase

New route/story replaces /solo as primary solo experience
Chapter dataNew shared/chapters.js — array of chapter configs (dialog, concept, objectives, hints, stock count, duration, scripted events). Adding chapters = adding data.
StoryPageNew screen component — state machine cycling through phases (story → learn → play → reflect). Renders Dollar Guy + dialog in story/learn phases, existing sim components in play phase.
DollarGuy componentNew component — speech bubble UI, renders your pixel art asset, handles click-through dialog sequences.
HintOverlayNew component — Dollar Guy's in-game hints during play phase. Positioned near relevant UI elements. Driven by chapter config.
ObjectiveTrackerNew component — shows current objective at top of play screen. Checks completion conditions from chapter config.
Reused as-isNewsEngine, StockCard, TradeControls, MiniChart, NewsTicker, Timer, sound engine, price generation — all unchanged.
ProgresslocalStorage saves completed chapters. Simple — no backend needed.
HomeScreen updateReplace "SOLO" button with "STORY MODE" that routes to /story. Show chapter progress (1/3, 2/3, etc.).
